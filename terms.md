## 00. Preamble & Core Principle
This specification defines the regulatory standard governing all hardware evaluations hosted by Lost In Tech. It serves to protect strict consumer-facing integrity, preserve full operational transparency, and eliminate inefficiencies or misunderstandings during the negotiation cycle and beyond.  
The provision of physical equipment for evaluation constitutes an asset-for-service exchange, balancing physical inventory against intensive **technical validation variables, diagnostic schedules, and un-curated media coverage**. This framework explicitly does not establish a marketing services agreement, a corporate campaign sponsorship, or a work-for-hire production contract.  

* Important: If your proposal or agreement is for **sponsored work**, you will need a different agreement. Please contact us. 



## 01. Articles of Engagement

### Editorial Autonomy & Creative Control
* **Exclusion of Pre-Publication Approval:** Lost In Tech does not under any circumstances provide draft video reviews, preliminary scripts, rough cuts, or contextual outlines to manufacturers or PR entities prior to public dissemination. Content lifecycles operate completely independent of corporate approval structures.
* **Unvarnished Technical Evaluation:** Final content assets will contain a full, unrestricted assessment of the hardware. Terms attempting to restrict, soften, or manage the tone of the content produced will not be entertained.
* **Competitor Benchmarking:** Direct comparative positioning against market alternatives is often a core requirement of objective consumer journalism. The absolute right to contrast, benchmark, and evaluate any hardware unit against competitor products is retained entirely and requires no brand validation.
* **Reputation & Liability Exclusions:** We explicitly reject clauses protecting a manufacturer’s corporate "reputation" or "brand equity." Engineering results, UI/UX findings, or other testing outcomes documented during laboratory testing will be presented transparently to the end consumer as and when we deem it to be of customer interest.

### Intellectual Property Allocation & Media Rights
* **Asset Ownership:** All intellectual property, copyrights, trademarks, and metadata associated with video assets, audio elements, thumbnails, digital files, and engineered 3D designs remain 100% the exclusive property of Lost In Tech.
* **YouTube Platform Restrictions:** To protect structural platform distribution dynamics, manufacturers and PR agencies are **strictly prohibited** from re-uploading, slicing, or hosting modified derivatives of the core video assets on the YouTube platform.
* **Embedding:** Native digital embedding of the original, un-altered YouTube URL is permitted globally.
* **Cross-Platform Syndication:** Manufacturers are granted a non-exclusive, **revocable** license to distribute unmodified or minimally modified short-form vertical cuts on alternative social media platforms (such as Instagram, TikTok, or X), contingent on explicit creator credit. The overarching meaning, technical conclusions, and context must not be misrepresented, altered, or modified (including via AI voice synthesis or selective phrase clipping).
* **Paid Media & Advertisement Exclusion:** The provision of evaluation hardware **does not** constitute or grant authorization for paid programmatic marketing deployment. Manufacturers do not have the automatic right to utilize creator imagery, video assets, voice, or branding inside paid advertising networks (e.g., Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager). **Paid advertising placement requires a separate, premium commercial contract.**

### Operational Timelines & Scheduling Embargoes
* **Technical Validation Window:** Comprehensive empirical evaluation requires proper bench testing time. Our operational timeline scales to approximately **1 month** from physical asset delivery, unless explicit alternative metrics are authorized prior to logistics processing.
* **Exclusion of Content Blackouts:** Lost In Tech will not accept structural scheduling restrictions or mandatory "blackout windows" dictating when competitive hardware options can be tested, mentioned, or featured on the channel.
* **Publishing Timelines:** While coordination with corporate launch milestones is managed as an administrative courtesy, engineering precision, data integrity, and exhaustive exploration will never be bypassed to accommodate an external corporate promotional schedule.
* **No Timeline Guarantee:** Review work is secondary to paid sponsor work. No guaranteed delivery date is given, nor is the timeline above a promise or binding agreement. 
* **Non Disclosure or Contractual Agreements** While we understand that products are subject to embargoes and non disclosure periods, we generally do not sign anything beyond very minimal NDA agreements that cover **only** the timing of release of information regarding the product. Any attempt to manipulate speech or content integrity through an NDA will be rejected, and the document will remain unsigned. 

### Technical Support Strategy & Data Integrity
* **Consumer Transparency Disclosures:** All content assets open with a uniform public disclosure statement along the lines: *"No financial compensation was exchanged, and the manufacturer maintained zero creative or editorial input."* To uphold legal and ethical standards, the testing workflow cannot be curated or managed by brand marketing divisions.
* **Direct Engineering Communication:** To guarantee strict data accuracy, we generally prefer to bypass public relations interfaces and benefitt from an active communication link directly to the manufacturer's **internal engineering or technical support tier**.
* **Roadblock Documenting:** If an unexpected hardware failure, firmware anomaly, or software glitch occurs during testing, the designated engineering team will be consulted for troubleshooting. The baseline anomaly, the diagnostics process, and the resulting engineering resolution will all be considered fair game for inclusion at our discretion, on our judgement of relevance inside the published review.

### Laboratory Logistics & Asset Management
* **Inventory Retention Policy:** Unless fully prepaid customs documentation, return shipping labels, and automated courier collections are fully processed and provided by the manufacturer prior to delivery, all assets are deemed a direct exchange for the Review provided by Lost In Tech upon arrival. They have no monetary or influential value beyond this and do not constitute payment for anything above and beyond the content described in this document. 
* **Asset Integrity Management:** Hardware components integrated into our testing facilities will not be distributed, loaned, or displayed to third parties prior to the global public launch of the main content asset. Once the specified work is complete subject to the retention policy unless collected or returned, we retain possession indefinitely with no restrictions to dispose or use as we see fit.
* **Publication Liability Limitation:** While we expect to deliver content on every pre-screened hardware assset we receive, the liability on non completion is limited to either an offer of return of the hardware at the manufacturer or entity's expense, or the amount arranged to be paid for the work, which is typically zero for a review.
* **Unsolicited Goods:** Hardware or items delivered without prior agreement will waive any right to the review process, and we will decide on a case by case basis how to process them. This does not preclude the possibility of a normal review, but may also lead to return at your expense or disposal.
* **Asset Condition Liability:** Reviewing is not always a non destructive process, and often requires processes that might leave the hardware in a different condition than it was provided. We take all care possible but ultimately can not compensate for any damages or losses to hardware in our possession caused through the normal process of reviewing.

## 02. Compliance Verification & Authorization
To execute a hardware evaluation deployment to our testing environment, corporate brand representatives and authorized PR agents must demonstrate formal compliance by completing the following sequence:

1. **Formal Affirmation:** Continuing with the review process is explicit agreement of the terms in this document.
2. **Overriding Terms:** Only if you have received overriding agreement terms in email or writing from us, do they override those in this document. Please retain those terms for reference. 
3. **Logistics Authorization:** Upon verified execution of steps 1 and 2, request our authorized corporate shipping and logistics data.

⚠️ **Operational Integrity:** Any later attempt to introduce external corporate contracts, parallel non-disclosure addenda, or modifications to these terms subsequent to asset delivery will immediately invalidate the project and terminate the media plan.
